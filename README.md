# BFY-and-Epigenetic-Aging-
Effects of a randomized controlled trial of unconditional cash transfers on epigenetic measures of aging and cognition in children and mothers

# BFY

The BFY study is a randomized controlled trial investigating the effects of unconditional cash transfers on child and family development outcomes in the United States.

Clinical trial overview:  
https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT03593356?intr=baby%27s%20first%20years&rank=1#study-overview

## Cohort Description

Baby’s First Years is a randomized controlled trial of cash transfers in early childhood in the United States. Launched in 2018, the project recruited 1,000 mothers and their newborn infants from 12 birth hospitals across four metropolitan areas in the United States: New York City, the greater New Orleans metropolitan area, the greater Omaha metropolitan area, and the Twin Cities of Minneapolis and St. Paul.

Shortly after giving birth, mothers were randomized to receive an unconditional monthly cash transfer of either:
- $333/month ('high-cash group', n = 400), or
- $20/month ('low-cash group', n = 600)

Cash transfers were initially provided for the first 40 months of the child’s life and were subsequently extended to the first 76 months.

During the age-4 university visit (July 2022 – August 2023), mothers were invited to provide saliva samples from themselves and their children for DNA methylation analysis as an optional study extension.
